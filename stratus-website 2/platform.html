The Policy Scorecard

Track Article 6 readiness and carbon market policy across jurisdictions

The Stratus Policy Scorecard provides structured assessments of national carbon market frameworks. It evaluates regulatory clarity, institutional capacity, and alignment with international standards—the factors that determine whether projects can be authorized, financed, and brought to market.

What the Scorecard Covers

Each country assessment includes:

  • Article 6 status: Participation in cooperative approaches, authorization processes, corresponding adjustment procedures
  • Regulatory framework: Domestic carbon pricing, offset programs, baseline methodologies, MRV requirements
  • Institutional capacity: Designated national authorities, approval timelines, stakeholder engagement processes
  • Market infrastructure: Registry systems, credit issuance processes, trading and transfer mechanisms
  • Investment conditions: Foreign investment rules, revenue sharing frameworks, contract enforceability

Scores are not normative. They reflect the current state of policy development and the degree of clarity available to market participants.

How It Works

Assessments are based on published regulations, official government statements, multilateral disclosures, and consultation with in-country experts. Scores are updated as policies evolve.

Countries are evaluated across five dimensions:

  1. Article 6 readiness
  2. Regulatory clarity
  3. Institutional capacity
  4. Market infrastructure
  5. Investment environment

Each dimension receives a rating that indicates the level of development and transparency.

Who Uses the Scorecard

  • Investors assess country risk and prioritize pipeline development
  • Project developers understand authorization requirements and regulatory timelines
  • Governments benchmark their frameworks against regional peers and identify areas for capacity building
  • Policy analysts track global trends in carbon market regulation

The Scorecard is available by subscription. Selected country profiles are published in our Research section.
