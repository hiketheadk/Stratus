For Project Developers

Navigate policy risk and structure viable carbon projects

Project developers face regulatory uncertainty in many jurisdictions. Authorization processes are opaque, timelines are unpredictable, and policy changes can affect project economics after significant capital has been deployed.

Stratus helps developers:

  • Assess country readiness before committing capital
  • Understand authorization requirements and timelines
  • Model project returns under different policy scenarios
  • Engage with government authorities and reduce approval risk
  • Structure partnerships that align with national policy objectives

Country Assessment

Before entering a market, developers need to understand:

  • Whether the country has an Article 6 framework in place
  • What authorization processes apply
  • How long approvals typically take
  • What revenue sharing or benefit distribution is required
  • Whether foreign investment is permitted

The Stratus Policy Scorecard provides this information in a structured format. It allows developers to compare jurisdictions and prioritize pipeline development.

Financial Modeling

We model project economics based on:

  • Expected credit volumes and verification cycles
  • Price assumptions across buyer segments
  • Transaction costs (government fees, verification, legal, registry)
  • Revenue sharing with host governments or communities
  • Policy risk and regulatory delays

Models can be adapted by project type, geography, and financing structure.

Government Relations Support

We help developers engage with host country governments by:

  • Identifying the appropriate regulatory pathway
  • Coordinating with designated national authorities
  • Preparing authorization applications
  • Clarifying benefit-sharing expectations
  • Addressing policy or procedural gaps

Access

Policy Scorecard access is available by subscription. Advisory support for specific projects is delivered on a consulting basis.
